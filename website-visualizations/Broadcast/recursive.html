🔄 Recursive / Quadrant-Based Broadcast Simulator

Algorithm Description:

Recursive / Quadrant-Based Broadcast: The grid is recursively split into quadrants (2D) or octants (3D). Each node that receives the message becomes responsible for broadcasting within its assigned region.

2D Example (4×4): (0,0) sends to (2,0), then (0,2), then (2,2). Then all 4 roots work in parallel: (0,0) sends to (1,0), (0,1), (1,1) within its quadrant, while (2,0), (0,2), and (2,2) similarly subdivide their quadrants.

3D: Each octant root sends to 7 targets sequentially: (+x,0,0), (0,+y,0), (+x,+y,0), (0,0,+z), (+x,0,+z), (0,+y,+z), (+x,+y,+z), then recursively subdivides.
